General Chair's Message

It is my pleasure to welcome you to the 2019 Network and Distributed System Security (NDSS) Symposium.

NDSS 2019 continues its growth with its largest program ever: the most technical papers in its history, four workshops, an exciting keynote, and poster session.

This year we have five co-located workshops: Workshop on Binary Analysis Research (BAR), Workshop on Decentralized IoT Systems and Security (DISS), Workshop on Measurements, Attacks, and Defenses for the Web (MADWeb), and Workshop on Usable Security and Privacy (USEC). The workshops encompass critical topics in network and distributed systems security, some established at NDSS (BAR, DISS, and USEC) and one new one this year (MADWeb). I'd like to thank Mathias Payer and Giulia Fanti, the Workshops Co- Chairs, and Karen O'Donoghue, Steering Group Co-Chair, for bringing together such an exciting set of workshops.

Building on recent successes, this year we're continuing the tradition of organizing a poster session to showcase both in-progress and exciting recent work in various aspects of computer security. Thanks are due to Adam Aviv and Gang Tan, the Poster Co-Chairs, for making sure we have an excellent poster program.

Due to increased interest in our field and in NDSS, we have seen another record number of papers submitted to NDSS -- nearly 100 more than the previous record -- but even better, we have had a significant increase in the papers accepted -- over 25% more than the previous record. As a result, many exciting papers on a wide range of computer security topics will be presented at NDSS this year. Selecting the papers is a task that involves many people and many hours of hard work. I'd like to thank Program Committee Co-Chairs Alina Oprea and Dongyan Xu for the tremendous amount of work they donated and the excellent job they've done in putting together this year's program.

New this year is the first NDSS Test-of-Time Award highlighting the NDSS papers identified as having the greatest scientific and/or practical impact that were published prior to 2009. I want to thank the Time-of-Time Award committee for their work efforts, including the current Program Committee Co-Chairs, Alina Oprea and Dongyan Xu, along with David Balenson, Z. Morley Mao, and Paul Syverson.

Many individuals have contributed to making NDSS a success, including everyone on the Steering Group, Organizing Committee, and the Internet Society and Association Management Solutions staff. I'd like thank all of them for contributing their time and effort.

NDSS is possible in large part thanks to our generous sponsors. I'd like to thank Baidu, Check Point Software Technologies, Cisco, Google, and Qualcomm. I also thank the Internet Society additionally for hosting the symposium and providing funds for our student grants.

Finally, thank you for participating in the symposium and contributing to making NDSS a success. wish you all an excellent NDSS 2019!

Trent Jaeger
General Chair, NDSS 2019
