Network and Distributed System Security Symposium
Proceedings 2007

General Chair's Message

I take great pleasure in welcoming you to the Fourteenth Internet Society Symposium on Network and Distributed Systems Security.  Change is in the air, and this year you may notice some pretty important ones.

The biggest change for NDSS is our shift to a 3-day format.  Our program has always been flexible, adapting to changes in the network security field.  The new format gives us room to accommodate our expanding research domain.  Our program stretches from canonical to current, and includes both the theoretical and the pressing need.  This scope is the essential beauty of NDSS.

We have also added a thread of invited talks, starting off with Dr. Vernor Vinge as our keynote speaker, with later talks, occurring throughout the program, including such luminaries as David Hutton, John Viega, Tom Liston, and Jim Hoagland.  These talks, together with our panel discussion, hold the promise of important perspectives on our work; changing the view sometimes helps.

This symposium is possible only through the hard work of many people.  The steering group, which I am pleased to co-chair with Lynn St. Amour from the Internet Society, expertly charts a course for the conference that keeps its focus current, relevant, and practical.  Tom Hutton continues to do a superb job of coordinating local arrangements. Terry Weigler, with help from Michele Estadt, is magical with the logistics; the meeting simply would not happen without them. Doug Szajda skillfully coordinated the publications as Publications Chair.  Finally, I am grateful for the masterful work of Karen Seo as Publicity Chair.

The quality of this conference directly depends upon the quality of the papers selected to appear.  Under the leadership of the Program Co-Chairs, Bill Arbaugh and Crispin Cowan, the program committee chose an extraordinary set of papers.  I thank Bill, Crispin, and the entire program committee, for their expertise and dedication.  I also thank the authors who submitted papers and the speakers who present; you are the core of this symposium.

I am also grateful for our sponsors: The Internet Society for overall sponsorship, the National Security Agency (NSA) for patron sponsorship, Google, Afilias, and the San Diego Supercomputer Center (SDSC) for silver sponsorships, the National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) and Microsoft Research for bronze sponsorship, and IBM Research and Komoku for contributing sponsorship.  I appreciate your dedicated support.

This is my final year as General Chair.  I have been honored to serve you, and I look forward to our continued growth together.

Eric Harder
National Security Agency
ejh@tycho.ncsc.mil
