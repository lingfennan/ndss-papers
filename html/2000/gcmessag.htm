General Chair's Message


Welcome to the seventh annual Internet Society (ISOC) Network and Distributed System Security (NDSS) Symposium! Each year, this symposium brings together researchers, designers, implementers, and users of network and distributed system security technologies to discuss important security issues, challenges, and solutions. The NDSS 2000 Program Committee has assembled an excellent mix of technical papers and panel presentations that describe promising new approaches to Internet-type security. Our world continues to become more dependent on the Internet, and the need for widespread deployment of usable and effective network and distributed system security solutions is increasing just as rapidly. NDSS 2000 is an opportunity to have open dialog, share experiences and ideas, and explore solutions that will protect this valuable resource.

This year's event has been organized by many people who have invested considerable time and effort. The NDSS Steering Group, which I co-chaired with Torryn Brazell from ISOC, has provided overall leadership to ensure the success of NDSS 2000. Carla Rosenfeld, Michele Estadt, and the staff at ISOC have done an outstanding job handling registrations and many other behind-the-scenes activities. Tom Hutton has again provided wonderful local arrangements. John Kochmar has coordinated inputs from the authors and panelists to assemble the hard-copy proceedings and the easy-to-use CD-ROM proceedings. Dave Balenson has kept us all forging ahead as he handled a multitude of publicity details. Doug Maughan worked overtime to develop a first-rate set of timely tutorials. The Program Co-Chairs, Gene Tsudik and Avi Rubin, and the entire Program Committee are to be commended for their creativity, and diligence in developing an excellent technical program. Only with the collective hard work of all these individuals are we able to make the symposium a success — thanks to all of you!

Overall sponsorship has been provided for several years by the Internet Society. In addition, we have received support this year from a number of corporate sponsors. Thanks to the National Security Agency Office of INFOSEC Research, BBN Technologies, Network Associates, IBM SecureWay, Zipee.com, NetScreen Technologies, the San Diego Supercomputer Center, CygnaCom Solutions, Daedal Enterprises, and AXENT Technologies for sponsoring NDSS 2000.

Finally, I would like to thank all of the authors who will present their papers, the panelists who will share their knowledge and experiences, and everyone who attends NDSS 2000 for your contributions and participation. I am confident that the professional contacts you make at this symposium, the presentations, and these proceedings will provide ideas and insights that you can apply to your own security efforts.
